Our Stories

Milestones of Faith, Grace & Community

1868

First known account

During a certain service held in preparation for the feast of the Kirimatiyagara Church, a clash occurred, which has been presented as the first historical account regarding the Kurukulawa St. Lazarus Church.

1869

Separation of Parishes

In 1969, under the Benedictine priest, Rev. Fr. William Kanjimanathan, our parish of Kurukulawa separated from the Dalugama Siyane Korale parish and became a distinct parish.

1875

Patron Saint

On August 29, 1875, the then parish priest of Dalugama, Rev. Fr. C. Chardano, named St. Lazarus as the patron saint of our church.

1887

The New Church Building

In 1887, following a suggestion by a parishioner that a new permanent building was needed for Kurukulawa to replace the old thatched-roof church, Rev. Fr. Pulicani cut the first sod and laid the foundation stone for the new church on October 5th of that year.

1926

The New Church Building Completion

The new building, which commenced construction after laying the foundation stone in 1887, was completed in 1926. It was then blessed and dedicated to the public by the then Archbishop, His Grace Most Rev. Antony Coudert, O.M.I.

1994

The Re-establishment of the Parish

On August 15, 1994, the Kurukulawa parish was re-established as an independent parish. Following its independence, Rev. Fr. Bernard Balou, O.M.I., a priest of the Oblates of Mary Immaculate, took charge as its first parish priest.

2001

Celebration 75 years of Devotion

In 2001, under the parish leadership of Rev. Fr. Amil Benedict Gomez, the Kurukulawa St. Lazarus Church celebrated its 75th Jubilee festival with great grandeur. The portico in front of the church today was constructed during this period.

Unknown

Even though there are no specific historical records mentioned, it can be said that from 2001 to 2024, under the guidance of various parish priests, the Kurukulawa church community was beautifully nurtured, reflecting both its splendor and spiritual growth.

2025

The 100th Jubilee was Commenced

Nurturing its people in the face of all challenges, the Kurukulawa St. Lazarus Church announced during its 99th annual feast service held in September 2025 that it will be celebrating its centenary Jubilee festival.
